Foundation's Core Skills

What are three new things you have learned about yourself and your ego as a result of the core learning?

I like to think I don't have an ego. Doesn't everyone? This isn't entirely true though. One thing I've learned is that if I'm not careful, a little bit of an ego can start to grow. I need to be aware of this and squash it back down. An ego isn't helpful to anyone so I try hard not to listen to it.

I learned the names of my values. As I'll discuss below this was challenging but super beneficial. By know the names I can more accurately recognise what they are and how they should affect my life.

I also learned to pay more attention to how I'm feeling. Previously if I was working on something and started feeling a little off, I would kind of ignore it and push through. By going through this core process I've learned it's not necessarily the healthiest option for your mental wellbeing. Now while coding I've tried to stay aware of what my body is telling me, and respond appropriately. Self care is important.

What are the role of values, empathy and self awareness in learning and programming?

In the learning and coding environment there is a lot of team work. Having empathy will allow you to put yourself in other people's shoes and get a sense of how they might be doing. In this intense environment it's important to look out for one another and make sure everyone is doing ok. Having empathy will allow you to do that.

Being self aware will allow you to reflect on your words or actions, and the effect they may have on others. It's also important for self care. As we must look out for each other, we must also look after ourselves. It's important to take stock of how you're feeling, make sure you're getting enough rest and aren't burning out.

Values are that moral compass. There are some challenging times ahead, but if you stick to your values you should be able to navigate through without too much stress or distress.

What has surprised you the most about the core learning?

How challenging it was. Initially I didn't think it would be too bad. Have a sit and a think and there you go. Turns out being introspective is a bit more involved than that.

What were the most challenging aspects of the core learning?

The first values and strengths section. Values for a me are more of a feeling thing. There's that little niggle in your brain when your values are being challenged. I've known I have values, but actually putting a name to what they are was really hard.

Why do you think we, a programming school, are spending so much time focusing on core learning in a web development bootcamp course?

We're working on the full package. To be a good developer knowing how to code well isn't enough. You need to be able to work with people, as a team. The core skills will help with that.

Does the time you spent studying core learning here feel like a waste of time? Should you have just used that time to practicing programming instead? Justify your answer.

No, for the above reason. Developing these core skills will be super useful. I had to work harder at the core exercises than I did with some of the coding ones!
