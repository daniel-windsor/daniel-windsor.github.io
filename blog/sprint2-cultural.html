Learning Reflections

What is your long term goal and/or career pathway?

At this point I'm still not 100% sure, as I'm not super up to speed with what opportunities are available. I envisage myself getting a job in a small to midsize company. I've done the 'work with heaps of employees where no one knows any one' and it gets old pretty quick. I want a place with a good environment. Also I think it's important to be working on something I'm interested in. A project I can really get invested in. Also staying local is important. I like Wellington. It's cool here.

What skills (non-technical human skills) would you like to see developed in yourself while at EDA?

Networking. The non-technical kind. I find it super hard to approach people and introduce myself out of no where. I'm much more likely to sit quietly in a corner and try not to make too much eye contact. Terrible. Once I have an in then it's fine, but that first step is just so hard.

Describe your own strengths and limitations

Team work! I've had a lot a practice in working as part of a team. My previous role was running a team so I got pretty good at the skills involved with this. Things like keeping the communication open between different members. Delegating tasks. Keeping people to due dates. A key one taking a lot of information being provided by a lot of people and use it to come to a decision. Some times it was a discussion and a group decision, sometimes it was 'this is how it needs to be'. Sometimes you just need someone to make a call, and lucky for me that was my job. Hopefully these skills will be of use through this process

The one part of team leading that I still struggle with is the conflict management. It's hard dealing with angry people. No one likes it.

The other thing I have struggled with is motivation. In the past there have been times (some times a lot of times) where I need to work on something, but there's also something more fun I could do instead. Not the best. So far though, because web dev is the something more fun, it hasn't been a problem. I'll aim to keep it that way!

Based on the above description, a commitment to how you will manage your workload in this programme. This should include - an explanation of how you will manage yourself to work productively and safely with other learners, facilitators and industry/community representatives.

First step is to pace myself and to know my own limitations. It's not super healthy to do eight or ten hours of work straight, so I'll make sure to give myself regular mini breaks. Quick stretch then let's keep going. Also stay hydrated and eat well.

Another key that I've touched on before is that good communication. Be open with others about how you're feeling. If things aren't going well, or if you're feeling overwhelmed let people know. Discuss with your cohort, with the staff. Get support if needed.

Then you've got the basics. Be kind. Be respectful. We're all in this together.

This is my commitment.

A commitment as to how and where you will seek help in a timely way

There are so many people available to offer support. The other members of my cohort. EDA staff. I have some programming friends. The internet. If ever I am super stuck help is not that far away. If someone is busy, find someone else! I think I have enough understanding to know the difference between 'I am struggling because this is a tricky task' and 'I'm struggling because I'm totally lost'. In the case of the latter, I'll ask for help. No point in mucking around.

I commit to this also.

A description of what you expect from the facilitation team

Support. A nudge in the right direction. I don't expect (or want) them to just hand out the answers all the time. You don't learn that way. I want to be guided to find the answers for myself. Key word - facilitator, not teacher.
